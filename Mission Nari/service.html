Beti Bachao Beti Padhao

Beti Bachao Beti Padhao is a flagship program initiated by the Indian government to improve the country's falling girl-child sex ratio. The scheme aims to raise awareness about the importance of girls' education and prevent discrimination against them.The program also promotes girls' health and nutrition while preventing gender-based violence. the government offers cash incentives to families with a girl child.
Mahila Kisan Sashaktikaran

The Mahila Kisan Sashaktikaran Pariyojana is an Indian government initiative designed to empower women farmers. The project intends to increase women's participation in agriculture and prepare them for leadership roles in the agricultural sector. Women farmers in this scheme are trained in many parts of farming, including seed production, crop management, and post-harvest management.

Ujjwala Yojana

The Ujjwala Yojana is a scheme introduced by the Indian government to provide free LPG connections to women from low-income households. The "Beti Bachao, Beti Padhao" (BBBP) scheme, launched by the Government of India, aims to address issues related to gender inequality and promote the education and well-being of girls. To apply for benefits under this scheme, you may need the following documents:

1. Application Form: Filled out application form for the scheme.

2. Proof of Identity: Aadhar card, voter ID, passport, or any other valid identity proof of the parents/guardian.

3. Proof of Residence: Aadhar card, voter ID, passport, ration card, or any other valid proof of residence.

4. Birth Certificate: Birth certificate of the girl child.

5. Income Certificate: Income certificate of the parents/guardian to verify eligibility based on economic criteria (if applicable).

6. School Admission Proof: Proof of admission or enrollment in a recognized school or educational institution for the girl child.

7. Bank Account Details: Bank account details of the girl child or the parent/guardian (for receiving any financial benefits).

8. Photographs: Passport-sized photographs of the girl child and the parents/guardian.

The Pradhan Mantri Ujjwala Yojana (PMUY) is a government initiative aimed at providing LPG connections to women from below poverty line (BPL) households. The goal is to reduce the health hazards associated with cooking on traditional fuels and to promote the use of clean cooking fuel. To apply for benefits under the Ujjwala Yojana, the following documents are typically required:

3. Proof of Address: Aadhar card, voter ID, passport, ration card, or any other valid proof of residence.

4. Passport-sized Photographs: Passport-sized photographs of the applicant.

5. Bank Account Details: Bank account details of the applicant for savings and receiving any benefits.

6. Ration Card:To establish the household and address details.

The initiative intends to provide financial help to pregnant women from low-income families to access quality healthcare during pregnancy and childbirth. Under this program, eligible women receive financial support for transportation and medical treatment during pregnancy and childbirth.


The Janani Suraksha Yojana (JSY) is a safe motherhood intervention under the National Health Mission (NHM), aimed at reducing maternal and neonatal mortality by promoting institutional delivery among pregnant women. The Pradhan Mantri Matru Vandana Yojana (PMMVY) is a maternity benefit program by the Government of India aimed at providing financial assistance to pregnant and lactating mothers for their first live birth. To apply for benefits under this scheme, the following documents are typically required:

1. Application Form: Filled out application form (Form 1-A) for the scheme.

2. Proof of Identity: Aadhar card, voter ID, or any other valid identity proof of the applicant.

3. Proof of Residence: Aadhar card, voter ID, passport, ration card, or any other valid proof of residence.

4. Bank Account Details: Copy of the bank passbook or bank account details for the transfer of financial assistance.

5. Mother and Child Protection (MCP) Card: Issued by the health center, confirming the pregnancy and antenatal check-ups.

6. Maternity Certificate:From a registered healthcare provider confirming the pregnancy.

7. BPL or Ration Card: If applicable, to verify economic status.

8. Birth Certificate of Previous Child: If applicable, for verifying the eligibility based on the number of previous live births (this scheme is for the first live birth).
